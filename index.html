Discipleship & Evangelism (DE) Group: Purpose and Goals

Purpose

The DE Group exists to cultivate a lifestyle of faithful discipleship and bold evangelism. Rooted in the Great Commission (Matthew 28:19–20), this group is a space where believers are encouraged, equipped, and sent out to make disciples who make disciples. We gather to grow in intimacy with Christ and share His love with the world.

Core Rhythms of the Group

D - Discipleship

  • Growing in Christlikeness through Scripture, prayer, and accountability. (Romans 12:2, 2 Peter 3:18)
  • o Practicing spiritual disciplines that shape our hearts and renew our minds. (1 Timothy 4:7–8)

E - Evangelism

  • Boldly sharing the gospel in our daily lives. (Romans 1:16, Acts 1:8)
  • Building intentional relationships with non-believers. (Colossians 4:5–6)
  • Encouraging one another in our evangelistic efforts through testimonies and prayer. (Philemon 1:6, Hebrews 10:24–25)

FACT Qualities: The Kind of People We're Looking For

  • Faithful: Reliable and consistent in their relationship with God and with others. Faithful people show up, serve, and obey even when it’s hard. (Luke 16:10, Proverbs 20:6)
  • Available: Available: Willing to make time for spiritual growth, community, and the mission of Christ. Availability is about prioritizing God over distractions and busyness. (Isaiah 6:8, Matthew 4:19–20)
  • Committed: Wholeheartedly devoted to Christ and His mission. This is more than just showing up to group—it’s a lifestyle. Commitment means following Jesus daily, loving the Body, and embracing the call to multiply disciples. (Luke 9:23, John 15:5, Romans 12:11)
  • Teachable: Teachable/Trainable: Humble, willing to learn, receive correction, and grow. A teachable spirit invites the Holy Spirit and the Body of Christ to shape their walk. (Proverbs 9:9, 2 Timothy 3:16–17)

Structure

Highs and Lows

A time to share life updates—both victories and struggles. (Galatians 6:2, Romans 12:15)

Highs: Praise reports and blessings experienced through God’s provision. (Psalm 103:2, James 1:17)

Lows: Honest prayer requests, challenges, and areas where we’re seeking God’s strength and wisdom. (James 5:16, Psalm 34:17)

Ins and Outs

Scripture memory and evangelism reports. (Psalm 119:11, Joshua 1:8, Mark 16:15, 1 Peter 3:15)

Ins: Scripture memory and application. Members will meditate on 1–2 verses every two weeks and reflect on how God is speaking through them and shaping their walk. (Psalm 119:11, Joshua 1:8)

Outs: Evangelism reports—sharing gospel conversations, outreach efforts, and moments of stepping out in faith to make Christ known. (Mark 16:15, 1 Peter 3:15)

HEAR Journal

o Each member will engage with God’s Word using the HEAR method to deepen personal devotion and share what the Lord is teaching them. The HEAR method stands for:

  • Highlight: Choose a specific verse or passage from your reading that stood out to you. Write it down word-for-word. (Psalm 119:105)
  • Explain: In your own words, explain what the passage means in context. What was the author’s intent? What is happening in the verses? (2 Timothy 2:15)
  • Apply: Reflect on how this passage applies to your life today. What is God saying to you personally through this verse? (James 1:22–25)
  • Respond: Write a short response to God. This could be a prayer, a commitment, or an action step you feel led to take. (Romans 12:1, Psalm 19:14)

This journal method helps you not only understand Scripture but also hear from God and live out His truth.

Long-Term Goal

By the end of this group’s journey, each member is challenged and encouraged to start their own DE Group. This multiplication model ensures that we are not just growing personally, but that we are reproducing faithful followers of Jesus who continue the mission. (Matthew 9:37–38, 2 Timothy 2:2)

FAQ

What if I’ve never shared the gospel before?

That’s okay! This group is designed to equip and encourage you. You’ll learn through practice, support, and example. (Matthew 10:19–20)

Do I have to lead a DE Group after this?

We strongly encourage every member to multiply what they’ve learned by starting a group. You won’t be alone—we will equip you step-by-step. However, if you don't feel ready or led to lead, you are welcome to rejoin this group or participate in another DE Group. (2 Timothy 2:2)

What if I miss a session?

Life happens. Let someone in the group know and catch up as best as you can. We’re here to grow together, not to be perfect.

Can I invite others?

Yes! We welcome others who are willing to commit to the group’s values and rhythms. However, to maintain depth and relationship, the group will stay within 5–7 members. Anyone new must understand this is not a casual Bible study, but a commitment to Christ-centered discipleship and evangelism.

How long does the group last?

The length depends on the book we choose to go through. We typically meet every other week and cover 1–2 chapters per session. The group continues until we complete the book together.
